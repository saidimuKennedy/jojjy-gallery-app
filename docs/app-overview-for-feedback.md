# Jojjy Gallery — App Overview (for external feedback)

A quick summary of what the app is and where it's headed, to share with an
outside party for input on making the experience wholesome for both the
artist and gallery visitors.

## What it is today

A personal online gallery site for a visual artist (Njenga Ngugi). Visitors
can:
- Browse artworks by series/category, view details, dimensions, medium, year
- View a media blog (videos, images, audio, external links) about the
  artist's process and exhibitions
- Buy available artworks, paid via M-Pesa
- Read an About page with the artist's bio and career history

Behind the scenes, the artist (as admin) manages artworks, series, and blog
entries — today this management panel is embedded directly on the public
About page, which we're in the process of separating out for security
reasons (see below).

## What's being added (in progress)

1. **Event tickets** — the artist can list their own events (exhibitions,
   shows) and sell tickets directly through the site.
2. **Merch** — branded/merch items (prints, apparel, etc.) sold alongside
   artwork, with size/color variants and stock tracking.
3. **Fan notifications** — an on-site news/updates page, plus email and
   WhatsApp announcements so the artist can notify their fanbase about
   upcoming events.
4. **A dedicated CRM** — a separate, internal admin tool (not part of the
   public site) where the artist and any staff manage artworks, events,
   tickets, merch, and announcements, with editable staff permissions
   (e.g. door staff who can check in tickets but can't edit pricing).
5. **Paystack** as the payment provider for the new ticket/merch checkout,
   alongside the existing M-Pesa flow.

## What we'd like feedback on

- The overall artist workflow: is anything missing for a solo/independent
  artist running events, merch, and fan communication themselves?
- The visitor experience: browsing, buying, discovering events — what would
  make a stranger become a returning fan or buyer?
- Anything specific to running this in Kenya/East Africa (payments,
  logistics, WhatsApp use, etc.) that we haven't accounted for.
- Any "good to have" features common in similar artist/gallery/ticketing
  platforms that we should consider for a later phase.
